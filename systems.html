1.3 District Heating and Cooling

District Heating & Cooling

District heating and cooling systems provide thermal energy to multiple buildings from a central source. Instead of each building having its own boiler or chiller, energy is distributed through a network of insulated pipes, significantly increasing efficiency.

1.4 Electricity Generation

Geothermal Electricity

Unlike heat pumps that move existing heat, geothermal power plants tap into deep, high-temperature reservoirs—often over 300°F—to generate electricity. This heat creates steam that spins a turbine connected to a generator to produce electrical power.

The Impact

  • ✓ Lower Energy Bills: High-efficiency heat pumps reduce monthly electricity and fuel costs.
  • ✓ Local Jobs: Building and maintaining these networks creates permanent, local construction and utility jobs.
  • ✓ Grid Stability: Shared networks reduce "peak load" demand, making the entire community's power grid more resilient.
1.5 & 1.6 Benefits and Costs

Community & Economic Benefits

Geothermal energy networks transition us from individual energy consumption to a model of shared community resources. By recycling thermal energy that already exists in our environment, we create a system that is both economically sustainable and environmentally responsible.

Project Target

Geothermal District Systems

A Geothermal District System captures, moves, and repurposes heat across a community. Instead of relying on combustion, this system uses the earth as a thermal battery to maintain a stable ambient temperature loop.

  • Capture: Reclaiming waste heat from grocery stores or data centers.
  • Move: Distributing energy via underground, horizontal closed-loop pipes.
  • Amplify: Using water source heat pumps to reach usable temperatures.
