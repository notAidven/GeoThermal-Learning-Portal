1.1 Introduction

What is Thermal Energy?

Thermal energy is energy associated with heat. When you heat up a substance, its atoms move faster and its thermal energy increases. Heat naturally flows from warmer substances to cooler substances through a temperature gradient.

1.1 Introduction

Heat Below the Surface

Geothermal energy is derived from the Earth's interior—heat below the surface. Every building needs to provide heating and cooling to maintain a healthy indoor environment. Ground-source systems use these stable temperatures year round.

1.2 Individual Home Heating/Cooling

Heat Pump Amplification

A heat pump moves thermal energy from one place to another to provide both heating and cooling. In the summer, it moves heat from inside to outside; in the winter, it pulls thermal energy from the outside in.
