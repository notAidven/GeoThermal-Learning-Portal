Geothermal Energy: The Heat Beneath Your Feet

To maintain a comfortable and healthy indoor environment, every home—every building—needs to provide heating and cooling. We use thermal energy to recycle the heat we already have.

How it works

Recycling Heat Through Networks

Instead of only transforming an individual home, we can implement systems that share resources across multiple buildings.

Capture

Heat is energy. We can capture it, move it, and repurpose it. Waste heat is purposefully vented from large buildings such as grocery stores, ice arenas, and data centers.

Move

Thermal energy can be carried to buildings by water in a network of underground, horizontal pipes. These pipes form closed loops that move heat where it is needed.

Amplify

Inside each building, a water source heat pump amplifies that moderate temperature water—about 50°F—to higher temperatures for space heating and hot water.
