Geothermal Energy: The Heat Beneath Your Feet

A Community Guide to Clean Thermal Networks

1. Geothermal Basics

Key Question: What is thermal energy?

Thermal energy is energy associated with heat[cite: 54]. As you heat up a substance, its atoms move faster and its thermal energy increases[cite: 54, 55].

Ground-Source Heat Pumps

Uses the earth as a heat source and sink; operates year-round due to stable underground temperatures[cite: 72, 73].

Air-Source Heat Pumps

Moves thermal energy between the inside and outside air; most common type[cite: 67, 70].
