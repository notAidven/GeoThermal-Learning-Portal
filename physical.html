Hardware 01

Capture Components

Heat is energy that we can capture and repurpose. In a thermal energy network, waste heat is purposefully captured from large-scale cooling loads such as grocery stores, ice arenas, and data centers rather than being vented into the atmosphere.

Hardware 02

Distribution Network

The thermal energy is carried to buildings via water circulating through a network of underground, horizontal pipes. These pipes form closed loops that act as a shared resource, moving energy from heat sources to heat sinks across the community.

Hardware 03

Indoor Amplification

Inside each individual building, a water source heat pump is used to amplify the moderate temperature water—roughly 50°F—to the higher temperatures required for space heating and hot water.

By recycling existing heat, these systems maintain a healthy and comfortable indoor environment with significantly less energy than traditional HVAC systems.
